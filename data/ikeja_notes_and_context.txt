NARRATIVE MENTIONS OF IKEJA IN THE REPORT (by section)
------------------------------------------------------
Executive Summary (Figures C and D):
- Figure C (DisCo invoices and remittances, 2022/Q4): Ikeja invoice ~NGN
  35,069m, remittance ~NGN 34,408m, performance 98% - the joint-highest
  remittance performance alongside Ibadan.
- Figure D (customer metering as at Dec 2022): ~1,298 thousand registered
  customers, ~764 thousand metered, 58.82% metering rate - the highest of all
  DisCos.

Section 2.5.1 - Energy received and contracted capacity (Table 1, Figure 9):
- 2022/Q3: energy received 466.99 MWh/h vs available PCC of 465.96 MWh/h
  (variance +0.22%, i.e. Ikeja took slightly more than its PCC).
- 2022/Q4: energy received 504.27 MWh/h vs available PCC of 511.81 MWh/h
  (variance -1.47%). In Q4 all DisCos took less than their available PCC.

Section 2.5.2 - Energy billed and billing efficiency (Table 2):
- Energy received rose from 1,031.11 GWh (Q3) to 1,113.40 GWh (Q4), an
  increase of +82.29 GWh (+7.98%) - among the highest increases of all DisCos.
- Energy billed rose from 914.45 GWh to 992.33 GWh.
- Billing efficiency improved from 88.69% to 89.12% (+0.43 pp); the report
  identifies Ikeja as having the highest billing efficiency of all DisCos in
  2022/Q4.

Section 2.5.3 - Revenue and collection efficiency (Table 3):
- Billings: NGN 46.55bn (Q3) -> NGN 51.81bn (Q4).
- Collections: NGN 43.40bn (Q3) -> NGN 47.42bn (Q4).
- Collection efficiency fell from 93.23% to 91.52% (-1.71 pp). Ikeja is named
  among the four DisCos (Jos, Eko, Ikeja, Kano) whose collection efficiency
  declined, though per Table 3 it remained the highest of the 11 DisCos in
  both quarters.

Section 2.5.4 - ATC&C losses (Table 4):
- ATC&C loss: 17.29% (Q3) -> 18.43% (Q4) against a 2022 MYTO target of
  11.37%. Per Table 4 this was the lowest (best) ATC&C loss of all DisCos in
  both quarters, but still above its allowed target; the report notes no
  DisCo met its loss target in either quarter.

Section 2.5.5 - Market remittance to NBET and MO (Tables 5 & 6, Figure 10):
- Q4 invoices: NBET NGN 32.22bn (full); MRO of 89.45% giving an MRO-adjusted
  final obligation of NGN 28.82bn; MO NGN 6.25bn.
- Q4 remittances: NGN 27.84bn to NBET and NGN 6.57bn to MO (combined NGN
  34.41bn against a combined MRO-adjusted invoice of NGN 35.07bn).
- Combined remittance performance: 108.05% (Q3) -> 98.11% (Q4). Ikeja is
  cited among DisCos with remittance performance above 90% in Q4 and also
  among the four DisCos with the largest declines (-9.94 pp).

Section 2.5.6 - Remittance to the Market Operator (Figure 11):
- Ikeja and Ibadan were the only DisCos with >100% remittance to MO in Q4:
  Ikeja 105.12% (NGN 6.571bn paid against an invoice of NGN 6.25bn).
  Performance above 100% reflects payment of arrears (report footnote 7).
- Q3 -> Q4 change: -2.97 pp (Q3 ~108% per Appendix IV).

Section 2.5.7 - Remittance to NBET (Figure 12):
- Q4 performance ~97% measured against the MRO-adjusted invoice (NGN 27,839m
  remitted against NGN 28,820m per Figure 12).
- Ikeja recorded one of the largest declines in NBET remittance performance:
  -19.03 pp (second only to Jos at -21.55 pp). Q3 was ~116% (Appendix V).
- The report attributes the sector-wide Q4 decline mainly to non-remittance
  of regulatory net-off funds by the Ministry of Finance, and notes that no
  DisCo had adequate collections to meet full upstream obligations without
  net-offs.

Section 2.5.8 / Table 7 - Bilateral customers:
- "IKEJA EGIN" (Ikeja/Egbin bilateral arrangement) is listed among bilateral
  customers with zero NBET and MO invoices and zero remittances in 2022/Q4
  (all values 0.00 / 0%).

Section 4.2 - Metering (Tables 12 & 13, Appendices X-XII):
- As at 31 December 2022: 1,298,323 registered customers; 763,680 metered;
  58.82% metering rate (highest of all DisCos).
- 52,177 meters installed in Q4 vs 28,733 in Q3 (+23,444 / +81.59% - the
  highest quarterly increase of any DisCo). Ikeja led all DisCos, accounting
  for 31.70% of total industry installations in Q4.
- All 52,177 Q4 installations were under the MAP scheme (32.59% of all MAP
  installations - the highest of any DisCo). Zero NMMP installations in Q4;
  Ikeja's NMMP Phase-0 allocation of 111,703 meters was fully installed by
  the end of 2021.
- Cumulative installations through Q4 2022: 454,729 out of 1,186,114
  contracted (MAP: 343,026 of 1,074,411; NMMP: 111,703 of 111,703).

Section 4.3 - Customer complaints (Table 14, Appendices VI & XIV):
- Q4: 29,282 complaints received (-2,762 / -8.62% vs Q3's 32,044); 19,536
  resolved; 9,746 unresolved; resolution rate 66.72% (Q3: 90.09%).
- Ikeja and Ibadan were the only DisCos below a 90% resolution rate in Q4;
  Ikeja's 66.72% was the lowest of all DisCos.
- Category breakdown (Q4): Meter 12,755; Others 8,453; Billing 2,696;
  Interruption 1,967; Delay 1,744; Disconnection 1,023; Loadshedding 359;
  Voltage 285.

Section 4.4 - Forum Offices (Table 15, Appendices VII & XIII):
- The Forum Office serving Ikeja DisCo received the highest number of appeals
  of any DisCo in Q4: 906 active appeals (550 brought forward from Q3 + 356
  new), of which 500 were resolved and 406 remained pending after 8 panel
  sittings (resolution rate 55.19%).
- Appeal categories: Billing 640; Meter 218; Others 26; Disconnection 16;
  Loadshedding 3; Interruption 2; Delay 1; Voltage 0.
- Forum Office contact (Appendix XV): Ikeja, Lagos State - 199 Obafemi
  Awolowo Way, Alausa, Ikeja, Lagos State; Tel: 8106807298;
  Email: ikejaforum@nerc.gov.ng.
  (A separate Eko Forum Office at 61 Odunlami Street, Lagos Island covers
  Eko DisCo.)

Section 4.5 - Health and Safety:
- Ikeja and Yola were the only DisCos that reported no incidents in 2022/Q4
  (0 incidents, 0 injuries, 0 deaths). IKEDC accordingly shows no bars in
  Figure 15 (incidence report by licensee).

DATA QUALITY / INTERPRETATION NOTES
-----------------------------------
1. Two bases for NBET remittance performance: the main report (Figure 12,
   Table 6) measures performance against the MRO-adjusted invoice (Ikeja Q4
   ~97-98%), while Appendix V's Q4 figure (86%) is measured against the full
   NBET invoice of NGN 32.22bn. Both are consistent with the same NGN 27.84bn
   remitted.
2. Table 13's column headed "Number of Metered Customers as at 2022/Q4"
   actually lists total REGISTERED customers (Ikeja: 1,298,323, matching the
   registered-customer figure in Table 12; actual metered customers were
   763,680).
3. Probable row misalignment in Appendix VII: the figures attributed there to
   "Gusau, Zamfara State" (Q3: 910 received / 360 resolved / 550 pending /
   39.56%; Q4: 906 / 500 / 406 / 55.19%) match the Ikeja Forum Office data in
   Table 15 and Appendix XIII, while Appendix VII's "Ikeja, Lagos State" row
   (15 / 1 / 14 / 6.67%) matches Appendix XIII's Jigawa row. The CSVs here
   follow Table 15 and Appendix XIII, which are internally consistent (550
   pending at end of Q3 = 550 brought forward into Q4). On that basis,
   Ikeja's Q3 Forum figures were most likely 910 appeals received, 360
   resolved, 550 pending (39.56%) - treat with caution given the misalignment.
4. Figures C, 10, 11 and 12 plot values in NGN MILLIONS even though the axis
   labels read "Billion" (e.g. Ikeja's 35,069 / 34,408 in Figure 10 = NGN
   35.07bn / 34.41bn).
5. Appendix IV and V column headers mislabel the periods as 2022/Q2 and
   2022/Q3; the months shown (Jul-Dec 2022) confirm the data are actually
   for 2022/Q3 and 2022/Q4.
6. Remittance performance above 100% (e.g. Ikeja's 105.12% to MO in Q4 and
   108.05% combined in Q3) reflects payment of arrears (report footnote 7).
7. Monthly values in the appendices are rounded, so their quarterly sums can
   differ slightly from the stated quarterly totals (e.g. Q4 energy received
   sums to 1,114 GWh vs the stated 1,113.40 GWh).
8. No Ikeja-specific entries appear in the generation (GenCo), licensing,
   captive-power, mini-grid or meter-service-provider certification sections
   for 2022/Q4. Two Lagos-located items that may fall within Ikeja Electric's
   franchise area but are NOT explicitly linked to Ikeja in the report:
   (a) Chi Limited's 23.59MW captive plant at Ajao Estate, Lagos (Table 9);
   (b) Enaro Energy Mini Grid Limited's 999kW permit at Ishoka phase 1,
   Mercy Land and Mercy phase 1 Estate, Ajobo Ipaja LGA, Lagos (Table 10).
   Egbin power station (referenced in the "IKEJA EGIN" bilateral) is a GenCo
   reported separately from Ikeja DisCo.
